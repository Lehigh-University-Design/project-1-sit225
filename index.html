Error1971

The Creeper virus is considered the first computer virus.
It infected DEC PDP-10 computers running the TENEX operating system, displaying the message:

"I'm the creeper, catch me if you can!"

Error1983

The term "computer virus" is coined by American computer scientist Fred Cohen. He also develops the first experimental self-replicating program to demonstrate the concept.

Error1986

The Brain virus, the first IBM PC-compatible virus, is discovered. It infects the boot sector of floppy disks and displays a message from the authors, two Pakistani brothers.

Error1987

The Vienna virus becomes the first known virus for MS-DOS. It is a boot sector virus that infects executable files.

Error1988

The Morris Worm, created by Robert Tappan Morris, spreads rapidly across the early internet, causing widespread disruption. Morris later became the first person convicted under the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act.

Error1990

The Michelangelo virus attracts significant media attention due to its potential to destroy data on infected computers on March 6, the birthday of the renowned artist Michelangelo. The Michelangelo scare leads to increased awareness of computer security, and antivirus software companies emerge to combat the growing threat.

Error1999

The Melissa virus spreads via email, affecting Microsoft Word documents. It quickly becomes one of the most widespread email viruses, highlighting the vulnerabilities of email systems.

Error2000

The "ILOVEYOU" virus, originating in the Philippines, spreads globally through email, causing extensive damage by overwriting files and sending copies of itself to contacts.

Error2003

The Blaster Worm exploits a vulnerability in Microsoft Windows and spreads rapidly, causing widespread network congestion.

Error2007

The Conficker worm infects millions of computers worldwide, highlighting the challenges in securing large-scale computer networks.

Error2010

The Stuxnet worm is discovered, targeting supervisory control and data acquisition (SCADA) systems. It is believed to be the first cyberweapon designed for cyber-espionage and sabotage.
